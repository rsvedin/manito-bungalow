Spokane Sports

Gonzaga Basketball — The Heart of Spokane

If there's one thing that unites all of Spokane, it's Gonzaga basketball. On game nights, the city turns blue and white. The energy at McCarthey Athletic Center — affectionately known as "The Kennel" — is electric, and the Bulldogs have become one of the most consistently excellent programs in all of college basketball.

Whether you're a lifelong Zags fan making a pilgrimage to The Kennel or you're visiting Spokane and want to experience the city's biggest cultural event, a Gonzaga basketball game is an absolute must.

The 2025–2026 Season

This year is historic. It's Gonzaga's final season in the West Coast Conference before the program officially joins the Pac-12 Conference on July 1, 2026. Under legendary head coach Mark Few — now in his 27th season — the Bulldogs are competing at the highest level as they prepare for their new conference home.

The roster features a talented mix of returning stars and exciting additions. Keep an eye on Graham Ike and Braden Huff in the frontcourt, Braeden Smith running the point, and transfer Adam Miller providing backcourt scoring. With the Pac-12 transition on the horizon, the team is playing with an extra level of intensity.

McCarthey Athletic Center — "The Kennel"

The Kennel is one of the most intimidating venues in college basketball. With a capacity of 6,000 and a student section that rivals any in the country, the atmosphere is absolutely electric. Gonzaga home games regularly sell out, and the energy from tip-off to final buzzer is something you have to experience in person.

McCarthey Athletic Center ("The Kennel")

Address: 801 N Cincinnati St, Spokane, WA 99258

Capacity: 6,000

Distance from Manito Bungalow: ~4 miles (12-15 minutes)

Tickets: gozags.com

Conference: West Coast Conference (final season; joining Pac-12 July 2026)

Game Day in Spokane

Game day in Spokane is an event unto itself. Here's what we recommend:

  • Pre-game: Head to Jack & Dan's Bar & Grill near campus — the unofficial pre-game headquarters for Gonzaga fans
  • Grab a beer: Hit one of Spokane's great breweries before the game
  • Post-game: The bars around campus and downtown will be buzzing after a win. It's one of the best nights out in Spokane

Why Stay at Manito Bungalow

At just 12–15 minutes from The Kennel, Manito Bungalow is an ideal home base for Gonzaga game weekends. With room for up to 10 guests, you can bring the whole crew. After the game, come back to a real home — not a hotel room. Relax on the porch, rehash the game over drinks in the living room, or take a morning walk through Manito Park to recover.
