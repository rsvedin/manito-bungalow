March Madness is here, and **Gonzaga is in the thick of it** — again.

As of March 5, 2026, ESPN's Joe Lunardi has the Bulldogs projected as a **No. 3 seed in the West Region** and the **No. 12 team overall** in his latest Bracketology update. After a brief dip to a No. 4 seed following a road loss at Saint Mary's in the WCC regular-season finale, the Zags have climbed back thanks to chaos among the teams around them.

## How Gonzaga Got Here

Mark Few's squad has put together another outstanding season in what is a **historic year for the program** — it's Gonzaga's final season in the West Coast Conference before officially joining the Pac-12 on July 1, 2026.

The Bulldogs sit behind only Purdue (No. 9 overall), Nebraska (No. 10), and Iowa State (No. 11) among projected three seeds. Meanwhile, the teams nipping at their heels have been stumbling:

- **Kansas** dropped to a No. 4 seed after losing at Arizona State — a team Gonzaga beat by 12 back in November. The Jayhawks have lost three of their last four.
- **Texas Tech** and **Alabama** both lost on Tuesday, giving Gonzaga breathing room.
- **Virginia**, the final No. 4 seed, was blown out by 26 at Duke over the weekend.

## What's Next

Gonzaga's next game is **Monday, March 9 at 6:00 PM on ESPN+** in the WCC Tournament semifinals. They'll face the winner of Oregon State and one of No. 5 San Francisco, No. 8 Washington State, No. 9 Portland, or No. 12 Pepperdine.

A strong showing in the WCC Tournament could push the Zags up to a No. 2 seed on Selection Sunday — which would mean hosting first and second round games.

## Players to Watch

Keep an eye on **Tyon Grant-Foster**, **Jalen Warly**, and **Davis Fogle** as the Bulldogs gear up for the tournament. Under Coach Few — now in his 27th season — this team has the experience and depth to make another deep run.
