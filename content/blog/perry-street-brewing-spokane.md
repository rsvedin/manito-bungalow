If you want to understand what makes Spokane's beer scene special, start at **Perry Street Brewing**. Tucked into the heart of the Perry District on Spokane's lower South Hill, this neighborhood brewery has quietly become one of the most awarded in the state — with three Gold Medals from the Great American Beer Festival to prove it.

## The Beer

Perry Street's tap list is where precision meets creativity. Their flagship beers are clean, balanced, and expertly crafted — the kind of beers that make you slow down and pay attention. They rotate their offerings regularly, so there's always something new alongside the staples.

Whether you lean toward hop-forward IPAs, malty ambers, or crisp lagers, Perry Street delivers. The Great American Beer Festival medals aren't a fluke — this is a brewery that takes their craft as seriously as anyone in the Pacific Northwest.

## The Space

The taproom is warm and inviting without trying too hard. It's the kind of place where you can post up at the bar solo or bring the whole group and settle into a table for a couple of hours. There's an easy, unpretentious energy here that reflects the Perry District itself — a neighborhood that's quietly become one of Spokane's best.

## The Perry District

What makes Perry Street Brewing even better is the neighborhood around it. The Perry District is a walkable stretch of South Perry Street lined with independent shops, cafes, and restaurants. The [South Perry Thursday Market](/blog/spokane-farmers-markets) sets up just steps away during summer. It's the kind of neighborhood that rewards a slow afternoon of exploring.
