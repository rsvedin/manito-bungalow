Here's something most visitors to Spokane don't know: the city operates **six public swimming pools**, and admission is **completely free**. All you need is a SplashPass — a free digital registration that works like a library card — and your family has access to outdoor pools all summer long.

If you're staying at [Manito Bungalow](/) with kids (or without — adults love pool days too), this is one of the best perks of a Spokane summer visit.

## The SplashPass

The SplashPass is Spokane's free digital pool pass. There's no cost — zero, nada — and registration takes just a few minutes online. Once you're registered, you can visit any of the six city pools. Think of it as Spokane's way of making summer accessible to everyone.

Register at [SpokaneParks.org/aquatics](https://my.spokanecity.org/recreation/aquatics/) before your trip so you're ready to swim when you arrive.

## The Six Pools

### Comstock Pool

The closest pool to Manito Bungalow, located in Comstock Park. A great neighborhood pool that's perfect for families.

### A.M. Cannon Pool

Located in Cannon Hill Park, this is another South Hill option that's close to the bungalow.

### Liberty Pool

A popular pool on the east side of Spokane with plenty of space for families.

### Shadle Pool

Located in the Shadle neighborhood in northwest Spokane.

### Witter Pool

A neighborhood favorite in the north part of the city.

### Hillyard Pool

Serving the Hillyard neighborhood in northeast Spokane.

## Hours & Season

**Season Opens:** Mid-June (typically around June 16)
**Open Swim Times:** Mon & Wed: 6:30–8 PM | Tue & Thu: 6:30–8 PM (check website for full schedule)
**Cost:** FREE with SplashPass
**SplashPass Registration:** [SpokaneParks.org/aquatics](https://my.spokanecity.org/recreation/aquatics/)
**Free Swim Clinics:** Available Saturdays in July for ages 3–15 (thanks to Spokane Parks Foundation)

## Free Swim Clinics for Kids

The Spokane Parks Foundation sponsors free swim clinics on Saturdays in July for children ages 3–15. It's a fantastic opportunity for kids to learn water safety and swimming basics — and it's completely free. Check the city's website for exact dates and registration details.
