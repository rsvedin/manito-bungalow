Spokane punches far above its weight when it comes to annual events. If you're planning a trip to the Manito Bungalow, here's what's happening throughout the year — and why it's worth timing your visit around one of these.

## Bloomsday Run — May

One of America's largest timed road races. Over 50,000 participants fill Spokane's streets every first Sunday of May on a scenic 12K course. Even if you're not running, the energy is electric and the city comes alive.

## Lilac Festival — May

Spokane's oldest and most beloved festival runs for a full week, culminating in the Armed Forces Torchlight Parade — one of the nation's largest nighttime parades. It's a Spokane tradition that goes back generations.

## Hoopfest — June

The world's largest 3-on-3 basketball tournament transforms downtown into Hooptown USA. 6,000+ teams, 450 courts, 225,000 fans. There is nothing else like it anywhere.

## ArtFest Spokane — June

Over 150 juried artists take over Riverfront Park each early June for one of the Pacific Northwest's most celebrated outdoor art fairs. A perfect way to spend a summer afternoon.

## Pig Out in the Park — September

Spokane's legendary Labor Day weekend feast at Riverfront Park. 200,000 attendees, dozens of local restaurants serving up their best, three music stages, and free admission. It's the unofficial end of summer.

## Green Bluff Fall Festival — October

When autumn hits the hillside farms at Green Bluff, it's pure Pacific Northwest magic. Corn mazes, pumpkins, fresh cider, and tractor rides every October weekend. Just 30 minutes from Manito Bungalow.
