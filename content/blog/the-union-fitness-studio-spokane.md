Just because you're on vacation doesn't mean you have to skip your workout. **The Union** is Spokane's premier fitness studio, and it's become one of our go-to recommendations for guests who want to stay active during their trip. Whether you're into cycling, yoga, or strength training, The Union delivers an experience that's worth getting up early for.

## The Classes

### Rhythm-Based Cycling

The Union's signature offering. These aren't your typical spin classes — they're high-energy, music-driven rides that make you forget you're exercising. The studio is dark, the music is loud, and the energy is infectious. Whether you're a cycling veteran or a first-timer, the instructors make it accessible and fun.

### Hot Yoga

If cycling isn't your thing, the hot yoga classes are exceptional. The heated studio helps you go deeper into poses while the sweat provides a detox effect that leaves you feeling incredible. Perfect after a day of [hiking the trails](/blog/best-hiking-trails-spokane).

### Meditation

For something more restorative, The Union offers meditation sessions that are perfect for unwinding. On vacation, you finally have the time — take advantage of it.

### Strength Classes

Full-body strength classes that complement the cardio offerings. Well-structured, instructor-led, and suitable for all fitness levels.

## Drop-In Friendly

You don't need a membership to work out at The Union. Drop-in classes are available, and you can also find them on ClassPass and Wellhub if you're already a member of those platforms. Check the class schedule online before you go.

## Why We Love It

The Union isn't just a gym — it's a community. The instructors are passionate, the facilities are spotless, and the energy is the kind that makes you want to push harder. For our guests who are staying at [Manito Bungalow](/) for more than a couple of days, a few classes at The Union can become a highlight of the trip.

Start your morning with a class at The Union, then reward yourself with a walk through [Manito Park](/blog/manito-park-guide) and brunch at [Remedy Kitchen](/blog/pubs-on-grand-boulevard-spokane) down the street. That's a Spokane morning done right.
