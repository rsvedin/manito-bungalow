Every beer city has that one brewery the locals guard jealously — the place they bring friends who "really know beer." In Spokane, that place is **Whistle Punk Brewing**.

## A Family Affair

Whistle Punk is a father-son operation, and that family ethos shows in everything from the beer to the atmosphere. There's no marketing gimmick here, no Instagram-bait interior design, no overpriced merch wall. Just excellent beer made by people who care deeply about the craft. They also operate a second location in nearby Millwood, extending their reach while keeping the same honest approach.

## The Beer

This is where Whistle Punk stands apart. While much of the craft beer world chases trends — hazy IPAs, pastry stouts, fruited sours — Whistle Punk leans into traditional styles and executes them with remarkable precision. Their lagers are clean and crisp in a way that reveals just how hard they are to brew well. Their ales are balanced and drinkable.

If you're the kind of beer drinker who appreciates subtlety over spectacle, Whistle Punk will be your favorite brewery in Spokane.

## The Taproom

The original taproom is in Railroad Alley in downtown Spokane — a cozy, no-frills space that feels more like a neighborhood hangout than a polished brewery destination. That's by design. You come here for the beer and the company, and both deliver every time.
